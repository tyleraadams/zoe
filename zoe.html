I am a Ph.D. candidate in Geography (human-environment, feminist, political) at Ohio State University, with a completed graduate minor in Women's, Gender and Sexuality Studies.

My scholarly interests include nature-society relations & political ecology, “drug war” geopolitics, feminist studies, socio-environmental justice, conservation & environmental change, and resource conflict in the Americas.

The two main research projects that I am currently pursuing look at how, in different ways, counter-narcotics politics impact people and the environment in Bolivia and Honduras.

In 2010 I earned my MA at Ohio State in geography. I earned my BA in Geography at UCLA in 2005, with a minor in political science.
